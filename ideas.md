# Amora Game Website Design Ideas

## Design Context
Creating a website for game.amora.cr that guides four distinct personas (Investors, Village Stewards, Residents, Prosperity Creators) through their unique journeys to participate in the Amora regenerative ecovillage community in Costa Rica.

## Idea 1: Organic Mystical Forest

**Design Movement**: Enchanted Naturalism meets Gamified Journey

**Core Principles**:
1. Layered organic depth through overlapping foliage silhouettes and parallax effects
2. Mystical atmosphere with soft glows, particle effects, and ethereal lighting
3. Journey-as-adventure metaphor with path-based navigation
4. Warm earth tones grounded by deep forest greens

**Color Philosophy**: 
Deep forest greens (#1a4d2e, #2d5a3d) as the foundation representing the jungle, with warm coral/terracotta (#E07A5F) as the heart-centered accent. Soft cream (#FDF8F3) for content areas, creating clearings in the forest. Gold accents (#D4A574) for achievements and milestones.

**Layout Paradigm**: 
Vertical scroll journey with illustrated forest scenes that transform as users progress. Each persona path branches from a central "tree of life" on the landing page. Content appears in organic, rounded containers that feel like clearings or glades.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Animated tree roots that grow and connect sections
2. Floating firefly/particle effects on hover states
3. Path markers styled as trail blazes or stepping stones

**Interaction Philosophy**: 
Every interaction should feel like discovering something magical in the forest. Hover reveals hidden elements, clicks trigger gentle growth animations, progress feels like walking deeper into an enchanted wood.

**Animation**: 
Slow, organic movements - leaves rustling, roots growing, light filtering through canopy. Entrance animations use fade-up with slight scale, mimicking plants emerging from soil. Transitions between sections use horizontal wipes with foliage textures.

**Typography System**:
- Display: Playfair Display (elegant serif for headings, evoking ancient wisdom)
- Body: Source Sans Pro (clean, readable, grounded)
- Accent: Caveat (handwritten feel for quotes and callouts)

## Idea 2: Warm Terracotta Village

**Design Movement**: Mediterranean Warmth meets Intentional Community

**Core Principles**:
1. Sun-baked warmth through terracotta, sand, and warm whites
2. Human-scale intimacy with photo-forward storytelling
3. Handcrafted authenticity through textured backgrounds and imperfect edges
4. Community-centered layouts that emphasize connection over hierarchy

**Color Philosophy**: 
Terracotta coral (#E07A5F) as the primary warmth, balanced by sage green (#87A878) representing growth and nature. Warm sand (#F4E8D9) as the primary background, with deep charcoal (#2C3E50) for text. Ocean blue (#5B8FA8) as a refreshing accent.

**Layout Paradigm**: 
Asymmetric grid layouts inspired by village architecture - different sized "buildings" (content blocks) arranged organically but purposefully. The four persona journeys are presented as four distinct "neighborhoods" accessible from a central village plaza (landing page).

**Signature Elements**:
1. Textured paper/canvas backgrounds with subtle grain
2. Hand-drawn line illustrations as decorative accents
3. Rounded corners with slight irregularity (not perfectly uniform)

**Interaction Philosophy**: 
Interactions should feel welcoming and human - like being greeted by a neighbor. Buttons have a gentle "press" effect, cards lift slightly on hover as if being picked up, progress indicators use organic shapes rather than rigid bars.

**Animation**: 
Gentle, welcoming movements - content slides in from the side like opening a door, images have a subtle breathing scale effect, transitions use soft fades. Micro-interactions include gentle wobbles and warm pulses.

**Typography System**:
- Display: Barlow Condensed (bold, confident, community-focused)
- Body: Nunito (friendly, rounded, approachable)
- Accent: Kalam (handwritten warmth for personal touches)

## Idea 3: Regenerative Blueprint

**Design Movement**: Ecological Modernism meets Systems Thinking

**Core Principles**:
1. Clean, purposeful design that mirrors regenerative systems thinking
2. Visual hierarchy through strategic use of white space and accent colors
3. Information architecture that reveals complexity progressively
4. Connection visualization through subtle lines and node relationships

**Color Philosophy**: 
Forest green (#2D5A3D) as the primary representing regeneration, with warm coral (#E07A5F) as the heart/action color. Clean off-white (#FAFAF8) as the canvas, with subtle sage (#A8C5A8) for secondary elements. Deep slate (#334155) for text and structure.

**Layout Paradigm**: 
Card-based modular system where each journey step is a distinct "module" that can be expanded. The landing page uses a radial layout emanating from a central purpose statement, with the four personas positioned at cardinal points. Internal pages use generous whitespace with content in focused columns.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Subtle connecting lines between related elements (like mycelium networks)
2. Progress indicators styled as growing seedlings or rings
3. Icons with organic, hand-drawn quality within clean containers

**Interaction Philosophy**: 
Interactions reveal depth and connection - hovering on one element highlights related elements elsewhere, expanding a section shows how it connects to the whole. The system feels intelligent and interconnected.

**Animation**: 
Precise but organic - elements enter with purpose, connections draw themselves, progress fills like water rising. Transitions use clean fades with subtle directional movement indicating flow.

**Typography System**:
- Display: DM Serif Display (refined, purposeful, grounded)
- Body: Inter (clean, modern, highly readable)
- Accent: Permanent Marker (for emphasis and calls to action)

## Selected Approach: Idea 2 - Warm Terracotta Village

This approach best captures Amora's essence as a heart-centered, human-scale community. The Mediterranean warmth aligns with Costa Rica's climate and the feminine leadership energy. The village metaphor naturally supports the four persona journeys as distinct "neighborhoods" while maintaining community cohesion. The handcrafted, textured aesthetic differentiates from generic tech/startup sites and communicates authenticity.

### Implementation Notes:
- Use Barlow Condensed for bold headings (already referenced in Amora's existing site)
- Implement textured backgrounds using CSS patterns or subtle image overlays
- Create asymmetric card layouts for the four persona paths
- Use warm photography from Costa Rica throughout
- Implement gentle, welcoming micro-interactions
